# Social Calendar (social-manager)

The content-planning engine for `/market social <topic|url>`. You dispatch the
`social-manager` specialist to build a 30-day, multi-platform calendar where every post is
ready to publish or hand to a social manager. Output goes to `SOCIAL-CALENDAR.md`.

## Before you start

Load `~/.claude/skills/marketing-team/references/playbook.md` and the brand brief (Rule 1).
Dispatch the **`social-manager`** subagent with the brand brief, business type (Rule 5), and
any fetched site content. Apply the playbook rules — don't restate them.

- **Brief gate (Rule 1):** `social` is generative. If no brand brief and no URL exists, STOP
  and offer to create a brief first (2 min) or proceed with stated assumptions. If a URL is
  given, fetch it (`analyze_page.py` or `WebFetch`) to infer voice, audience, and themes.
- **Honesty (Rule 3):** present engagement/reach as labeled benchmark ranges, never a
  fabricated "this will get X followers."

## Phase 0 — Trends data (tiered)

Topical, current content beats evergreen filler. Before generating:
- **Tier 1 if connected:** use `ToolSearch` (e.g. "social trends", "twitter", "trending
  videos", "instagram") to find a connected social-trends MCP (X/Twitter, vidIQ-style) and
  prefer it for trending topics, formats, and sounds in the brand's niche.
- **Fallback:** `WebSearch` for current trends and platform best practices.
- **Label the tier**, e.g. `Data: trending topics (X MCP)` or
  `Data: WebSearch trend reads — connect a social-trends tool for live trend data`.

There may be a helper at `~/.claude/skills/marketing-team/scripts/social_calendar.py` for
scaffolding the calendar structure; use it if present, otherwise build the calendar inline.

## Phase 1 — Platforms & pillars

- **Pick 2–3 primary platforms** for the business type (LinkedIn for B2B/SaaS/agency; IG/TikTok
  for consumer/creator; YouTube for education/demos; Facebook for local/community). Focus the
  calendar there rather than spreading thin.
- **Define 4–5 content pillars** with a mix ratio: ~40% Educational, 20% Behind-the-Scenes,
  15% Social Proof, 15% Engagement, 10% Promotional. Ground pillars in the brand brief's
  offers and audience.

## Phase 2 — Craft the content

- **Hooks** — give platform-specific opening lines (the first line / first 3 seconds decides
  scroll vs read). Tailor to the brand's voice; show real examples, not placeholders (Rule 4).
- **Hashtags** — tiered per post: 3–5 niche + 3–5 medium + 2–3 broad + 1 branded. Counts:
  IG 5–15, LinkedIn 3–5, X 1–2, TikTok 3–5.
- **Engagement tactics** — end ~30% of posts with a question; native polls 1–2×/week; 1–2
  debate/storytelling posts/week.
- **Repurposing (1→10)** — turn one pillar piece (blog/video/podcast) into ~10 posts across
  platforms, scheduled over 2 weeks.

## Phase 3 — 30-day calendar

Generate a complete day-by-day calendar. Per post: platform, pillar, hook, full copy
(LinkedIn 150–300 words; X ≤280 chars; IG caption 100–200 words + visual/slide description),
hashtags, post time, format. Distribution rules:
- Each pillar appears ≥6× across the month.
- Promotional content never two days in a row.
- Engagement posts spread every 2–3 days.
- Mix formats (not all text, not all carousels); leave flexible slots for live trends with
  adaptation guidance.
